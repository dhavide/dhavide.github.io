Teaching has been a big part of my professional life for over 20 years. Working with students to build a shared understanding of deep and difficult concepts was one of the best parts of being a professor. In 2011, I was awarded the UOIT Celebrate Teaching! Award in recognition of my academic contributions at UOIT. In total, I developed over a dozen courses at UOIT—some undergraduate, some postgraduate—in both computer science and mathematics. While at Anaconda, I oversaw the creation of a number of successful online courses about using Python for data science. More recently, I have worked as an independent contractor for a variety of companies delivering data science training as well as designing instructional curricula and materials for companies like Anaconda, Quansight, and the Emeritus Institute of Management.

As a volunteer instructor for the Software Carpentry Foundation, I have taught at over a dozen Software Carpentry bootcamps aimed at bolstering the computational skill set of scientific researchers needing a boost. I have also participated in various Science education conferences (including the International Conference on Mathematics and Science Education in Developing Countries (ICMSEDC)).
