Teaching has been a big part of my professional life for over 20 years. Constructing a shared understanding of deep concepts was one of the best parts of being a professor. In 2011, I was awarded the UOIT Faculty Teaching Award in recognition of my academic contributions at UOIT. In total, I developed over a dozen courses at UOIT---some undergraduate, some postgraduate---in both computer science and mathematics. More recently, while at Anaconda, I oversaw the creation of a number of successful online courses about using Python for data science as well as the Anaconda Certification Program.

I have been involved as a volunteer instructor for the Software Carpentry Foundation; I have taught at over a dozen Software Carpentry bootcamps aimed at bolstering the computational skill set of scientific researchers needing a boost. I have also participated in various Science education conferences (including the International Conference on Mathematics and Science Education in Developing Countries (ICMSEDC)).
